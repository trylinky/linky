As a guideline, aim for around **four to seven** links in your bio. Few enough that the page stays scannable, but enough to cover your key destinations. The exact number matters less than the order: whatever you most want people to do should sit at the very top.

## Why fewer is usually better

When a page offers too many choices, visitors often pick none - a effect sometimes called choice paralysis. A short, ordered list makes the decision easy and lifts your click-through rate.

## How to decide what stays

- **Keep** your single top priority, your shop or main offer, a way to follow or subscribe, and your most important social links.
- **Cut** anything outdated, duplicated, or rarely clicked.
- **Group** related links so the page reads in clear sections rather than one long pile.

## Let the data decide

If you're unsure, turn on [analytics](/i/learn/how-to-track-link-in-bio-clicks) and watch which links actually get tapped. Promote the winners, remove the dead weight, and revisit every month.
