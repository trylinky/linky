A great link in bio is focused, on-brand, and kept up to date. Follow a few simple best practices and your page will do far more than a plain list of links.

## The essentials

- **Lead with one clear action.** Decide the single most important thing you want visitors to do and put it first.
- **Keep it short.** Aim for a handful of well-chosen links rather than everything you've ever made.
- **Use descriptive labels.** Say what each link is and what happens when it's tapped.
- **Stay on brand.** Match your [colours, fonts, and photo](/i/learn/how-to-customise-your-link-in-bio-page) to the rest of your presence so the page feels like *you*.
- **Keep it fresh.** Update the top of your page whenever you post something new, or use [live blocks](/i/integrations) that update on their own.
- **Capture an audience you own.** Add an [email sign-up](/i/learn/how-to-collect-emails-from-your-link-in-bio) so a visit can turn into a subscriber.
- **Track and refine.** Use [analytics](/i/learn/how-to-track-link-in-bio-clicks) to see what works and adjust.

## A few things to avoid

Don't overload the page with links, don't leave broken or outdated ones, and don't settle for a generic template that looks like everyone else's. Pick a [template](/i/templates) you can make your own and start [free](/i/pricing).
