A good link-in-bio username is short, memorable, and consistent with the handle you already use on social media. Your username becomes part of your URL - `lin.ky/yourname` - so it's worth getting right before you start sharing it.

## What to aim for

- **Match your existing handle.** If you're `@yourname` on Instagram and TikTok, use `yourname` here too so people recognise it instantly.
- **Keep it short.** Shorter URLs are easier to remember, say out loud, and print on packaging or a business card.
- **Avoid numbers and special characters** where you can - `yourname` reads better than `your_name_123`.
- **Use your real name or brand name.** It's the most professional choice and the easiest for people to find.

## Before you commit

Claim your username as early as you can, since good ones go quickly. Once your page is live you'll be putting that URL everywhere - your social bios, email signature, and content - so pick something you'll be happy with long term.

If you later outgrow the default URL, you can connect a [custom domain](/i/learn/can-i-use-my-own-domain) on a paid plan to use your own web address instead. [Claim your username](/i/pricing) for free to get started.
