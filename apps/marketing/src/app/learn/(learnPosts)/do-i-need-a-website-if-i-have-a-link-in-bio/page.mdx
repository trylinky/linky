For many creators and small businesses, a link in bio is all you need - and for some, it's better than a traditional website. It loads instantly, works perfectly on a phone, and takes minutes to set up and update. If your goal is to point followers to your content, shop, and socials, a link-in-bio page does that job directly.

## When a link in bio is enough

- You're a **creator, musician, or artist** sharing content and links.
- You run a **small or local business** that mainly needs hours, contact, and a few links.
- You want something **fast to update** that always looks good on mobile.

## When you might also want a full website

- You need **lots of pages** of content (a large blog, documentation, a big catalogue).
- You want **deep SEO** for many keywords, or fully custom functionality.
- You're running a **store with many products** and checkout.

The good news: these aren't mutually exclusive. Many people use a link-in-bio page as their mobile-first front door and link out to a deeper site where needed. With a [custom domain](/i/learn/can-i-use-my-own-domain), your link-in-bio page can even *be* your website at your own address. Start [free](/i/pricing).
